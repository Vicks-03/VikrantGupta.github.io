About Me

I'm an undergraduate physics student at IISER Pune, pursuing an integrated Master's degree in physics. My journey through science has been driven by genuine curiosity about how the universe works at the largest scales. I find deep satisfaction in understanding the fundamental principles that govern galaxies, dark matter, and the structure of the cosmos. What fascinates me most is how we can take observations of distant galaxies and use them to understand cosmic evolution. I enjoy building Python scripts and models to analyze astrophysical data, turning raw observations into insights. This website exists as a personal space to document my learning journey and share the projects and ideas that excite me. Beyond physics, I love reading — particularly philosophy and science history that explore how we came to understand the universe. I play guitar, enjoy hiking in the mountains, and find that time in nature often brings clarity to complex ideas.

Projects

Isolating the Dependence of Integrated Galaxy SED Flux on Stellar Population Age

Project Report December 2025

An independent project examining how variations in stellar population age affect the integrated spectral energy distributions of galaxies, using controlled modelling approaches to isolate and interpret age-dependent trends.

Review of “The Impact of Parametric Star Formation History Models on Galaxy Physical Parameter Estimates”

Paper Review December 2025

A review examining how commonly used parametric star-formation history models influence galaxy properties inferred from SED fitting. The review focuses on the role of priors, model bias, and the limitations of broad-band photometry in recovering realistic galaxy formation histories.

Review of “How to Measure Galaxy Star Formation Histories II: Nonparametric Models”

Paper Review December 2025

A review of how nonparametric star formation history models are used to infer galaxy ages, masses, and star formation rates from photometric data, with emphasis on the role of priors.

Review of “Stellar Population Inference with Prospector”

Paper Review December 2025

A review of the Prospector SED-fitting framework, focusing on how Bayesian inference and flexible star formation history models are used to extract physical galaxy properties from photometric and spectroscopic data.

Review of “Extraordinarily Bright Lensed Galaxy at Redshift 5.04”

Paper Review December 2025

A review of a strongly lensed galaxy at redshift z = 5.04, exploring how gravitational lensing enables precise measurements of stellar mass, star formation, and dust properties in the early universe.
